Seismic activity and the Drifiting Indian Plate

About 140 million years ago, Gondwana (The Super Continent) broke into Antarctic, African, Australian and the Indian Plate. The Indian Plate drifting north collided with Eurasian plate,giving rise to the Himalayas.

The cause of major earthquakes in these regions is due to the active movement of Indian Plate northwards by 5cm per year. These collisions are cause of seismic activities.

Assam-Tibet Earthquake 1950

Magnitude: 8.6

Location: Between Assam and Tibet

The largest recorded quake by Continental Collision obliterated many villages. Led to landlsildes, rising levels of river waters and consequently floods.

Nepal-Bihar Earthquake 1934

Magnitude: 8.0

Location: 9.5 km south of Mount Everest

One of the worst Earthquakes in Nepal history . Sand and Water vents appeared throughout central vents of the earthquake area.

Sagaing Earthquake 1946

Magnitude: 8

Location: Sagaing Fault, Mayanmar

Between 1930 and 1956, six M7.0+ earthquakes occurred near the right-lateral Sagaing Fault, resulting in severe damage in Myanmar including the generation of landslides, liquefaction and the loss of 610 lives.
